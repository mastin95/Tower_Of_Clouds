# History & Cosmology Framework

## The Shape of Time

Before writing history, decide the world's **temporal philosophy**:

- **Cyclical**: History repeats. Ages rise and fall. The present is an echo.
- **Degenerative**: The world was better before. Something was lost. Decline is the arc.
- **Ascending**: Things get better, but at a cost. Progress is real but corrupting.
- **Fragmented**: History is contested. There is no single truth — only factions with memories.

The temporal philosophy should echo in the world's myths, politics, and the player's emotional experience.

## Creation Myth Structure

A strong creation myth isn't backstory — it's a **live argument** the world is still having. It should:

1. **Explain the central wound** — what went wrong at the beginning that everything else is a response to?
2. **Justify current power structures** — who benefits from people believing this version of the origin?
3. **Plant irresolvable questions** — what about the creation myth doesn't quite add up?
4. **Have a heretical counter-version** — what do the oppressed or outcast believe instead?

### Creation Myth Template

```
**The Orthodox Version:** [What the dominant culture believes about the origin of reality]
**The Wound:** [What fundamental rupture or loss defines the current state of the world]
**The Heresy:** [What an opposing faction believes — and why it's dangerous]
**The True Horror:** [Optional — what's actually true, if different from both versions]
**Live Consequences:** [How does this myth affect daily life, politics, or magic RIGHT NOW?]
```

## Designing Historical Events

History in worldbuilding is not a timeline — it's a **set of unresolved arguments**. Every major historical event should have:

- **Multiple interpretations**: Who tells this story, and what do they gain?
- **A living legacy**: How does this event affect the present? What wound does it leave?
- **A lie**: What is commonly believed about this event that isn't true?
- **A consequence no one talks about**: What did this event produce that everyone pretends didn't happen?

## Cosmological Structure

When designing the shape of reality:

- **Start with metaphysics, not geography**: What are the fundamental *rules* of existence in this world?
- **Make the cosmology feel**: A flat disc under a glass sky feels different from an infinite fractal void. What emotion does your cosmology produce?
- **Anchor the cosmology in the central theme**: If the world is about the cost of faith, the cosmology should make faith feel *structurally necessary* — not just culturally important.

### Key cosmological questions:
- What happens when mortals die?
- Is there a force that *opposes* the divine — and is it evil, or just different?
- Are the laws of reality stable, or eroding?
- Can mortals affect the structure of reality itself? At what cost?

## The Eras Framework

Organize history into **Eras** defined by a dominant force or rupture, not by dates:

| Era Name | Defined By | How It Ended | What It Left Behind |
|---|---|---|---|
| The [Name] Age | [What force shaped it] | [What broke it] | [Scar or gift to the present] |

Limit to 3–5 eras. More than that becomes noise.

---

## Thematic Resonance Check

- Does your cosmology make the world's themes feel *inevitable*?
- Does history feel like it's still happening — like its wounds are still open?
- Does the creation myth create genuine mystery, not just backstory?
